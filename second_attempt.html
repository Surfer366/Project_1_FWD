Introduction¶

Data Description: The data comes from the FBI's National Instant Criminal Background Check System. The NICS is used by to determine whether a prospective buyer is eligible to buy firearms or explosives. Gun shops call into this system to ensure that each customer does not have a criminal record or isn’t otherwise ineligible to make a purchase.

The NICS data is found in one sheet of an .xlsx file. It contains the number of firearms checks by month, state, and type. The U.S. census data is found in a .csv file. It contains several variables at the state level. Most variables just have one data point per state (2016), but a few have data for more than one year.

My reseach questions:

what is the average number of gun permits issued for each state? and by how much does it fluctuate overtime?

Is poverty related to gun purchases patterns?

What is the most popular gun type, and what are the implications of such?

What is the relationship between gun permits, gun type and geographic location?

Limitations¶

The data set was provided in the form of two seperate files, one of type .csv and the other of type .xlsx, which presented challenges executing the analysis.

There has been approximately 25% null values in the census_data file, this is quite high proportion and would weigh down on the quality of the analysis and consequently the conclusions.

An explanation of the definitions of the variables of the gun_data file was not presented in the dataset, as a result, describing the relationships articulary was challenging.
